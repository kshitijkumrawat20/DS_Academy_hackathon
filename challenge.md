All that you need to know about India Predicts 2026 — The National Election Prediction Challenge Powered by Data Science Academy
1. About the Competition

India Predicts 2026 is an election prediction competition organised by Data Science Academy (DSA), in association with Curvelogics. It is open to eligible individuals and teams across India.

Participants predict the winning candidates in assembly constituencies across five states — Kerala, Tamil Nadu, West Bengal, Assam, and Puducherry state elections by April 30th. Results are benchmarked against official declarations by the Election Commission of India (ECI) on May 4, 2026.

This is not a political exercise. Participants treat elections as large-scale human behaviour patterns that can be analysed through data and tested against a verifiable outcome. The first prize winner bags a reward of Rs 1,00,000, followed by Rs 10,000 and Rs 5,000 rewarded to second and third prize winners respectively

Participants predict the winning candidates for each assembly constituency across all five states. Predictions are submitted as an Excel file, the template of which will be sent to qualified registrants.

5. Scoring & Accuracy

Your accuracy score is the percentage of constituencies predicted correctly. A correct prediction is binary — win or lose. Withheld results will not be considered for evaluation.

Example: Predicting 140 Kerala constituencies and getting 112 right = 80% Kerala accuracy. Aggregate accuracy is the simple average (not weighted) accuracy calculated across all states.

5.1 Disputed Seats & Special Scenarios

On May 4th, we will fully align with the official declaration by the Election Commission of India, and if any constituency undergoes a re-poll or faces a dispute, that constituency will be excluded from the final evaluation of accuracy.

If the Election Commission of India postpones the election result declaration, we will adopt the updated declaration date in place of May 4th for all evaluations.

6.3 Tiebreaker Rules

If two or more participants finish with the same aggregate accuracy score, the following rules apply in order until the tie is broken:

Step 1 — Submission timestamp: The participant who submitted earlier ranks higher. This applies to the timestamp of the final valid submission. Example: Participant A's last submission was April 18 at 3:00 PM. Participant B's was on April 27 at 11:45 PM. Participant A ranks higher.
Step 2 — Breadth at 75% or above. The participant who has achieved an accuracy of 75% or above across a greater number of states ranks higher. A participant who crossed the 75% threshold in four states outranks one who crossed it in two, even if their overall accuracy is identical. Example: Participant A and Participant B both finish at 82% aggregate. Participant A hit 75%+ in four states. Participant B hit 75%+ in two states. Participant A ranks higher.
Both A and B have the same overall score of 82%; tie break condition: number of states with greater than 75% accuracy 
Step 3 — Accuracy in the largest state predicted. If the tie remains, the participant with the higher accuracy in their single largest state by constituency count ranks higher. Example: Both participants hit 75%+ in the same number of states. Participant A predicted West Bengal (294 constituencies) at 84%. Participant B predicted West Bengal at 79%. Participant A ranks higher.
Step 4 — Methodology quality. If the tie remains after Step 3, the DSA team will assess the methodology notes of the tied participants. The more original, well-reasoned, and clearly documented methodology ranks higher. This assessment is at DSA's sole discretion and is not subject to appeal.
Step 5 — Organiser discretion If the tie cannot be resolved after all four steps above, DSA reserves the right to split the prize equally between tied participants or make a final determination at its discretion
The guiding principle is straightforward: participants who submitted earlier are ranked higher before any other tiebreaker applies. If the tie still remains, participants who predicted all five states and maintained accuracy above 75% across more of them are rewarded next. Breadth of accurate prediction is valued over narrow high performance
